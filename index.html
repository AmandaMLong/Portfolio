Climate Change and Disease Ecology at the Southern End of the Americas

×

As a Research Fellow for the Sub-Antarctic Biocultural Conservation Program, I investigated the influence wetland and forest structure have on the prevalence of avian malaria in the context of global climate change to support policymaking in the UNESCO Cape Horn Biosphere Reserve.

I actively inventoried the southernmost forests of the world, the Magellanic Sub-Antarctic Evergreen Rainforest (49-56°S), to assess the relationship between carbon storage and avian richness using point count estimates

Skills

Biomass, Carbon Storage, Forest Inventory, Timber Metrics, Volume

Software

ArcGIS Pro, Excel, R

Data

Avian Richness, GPS, Tree DBH, Height, Percent Cover, Species

Positive Responses to the Global Tiger Recovery Program

×

As author on a publication in prep, Tiger (Panthera tigris) Endangerment: Positive Responses to Tiger Conservation Efforts, I reviewed pertinent literature to collect fecundity and survivorship data for 3 distinct tiger subpsecies: Amur (P.t. altacia), Bengal (P.t. tigris), and Sunda (P.t. sumatrae).

As a flagship and umbrella species, assessing the PVA of tigers aids in the assessment of global conservation efforts and understanding the overall health, integrity, and sustainability of larger ecosystems (Global Tiger Recovery Program 2010).

Skills

Attribute Selection, Dissolve, Population Viability Analysis

Software

ArcGIS Pro, Excel, R

Data

IUCN Geographic Ranges, Fecundity, Survivorship

Greater Sage-grouse as an Umbrella Species

×

As an author on a publication in review, Greater Sage-Grouse are an Umbrella Species for Shrub-Steppe Bird Communities at Range-Wide Scales, I used the SaveBirds app to mine Breeding Bird Survey data and investigate the influence greater sage-grouse (GSG) habitat management has on metrics of bird diversity over time.

Specifically, I analyzed avian richness, diversity, and rarity weighted richness inside and outside of managed GSG habitat from 1995-2019.

This research helped land managers and conservationists better plan to protect GSG as an umbrella species in order to protect avian biodiversity long-term.

Skills

Avian Diversity, Land & Wildlife Management, Spatial Analysis

Software

ArcGIS Pro, Excel, Python, R

Data

Breeding Bird Survey, SaveBirds

The Steppingstones of Dispersal in Felidae

×

For my undergraduate research at Central Michigan University, The Steppingstones of Dispersal in Felidae, I reviewed pertinent literature to collect dispersal data for the nine species of Felidae classified as endangered, vulnerable, and near-threatened.

I developed critical thinking and troubleshooting skills while conducting independent research with an advisor that challenged me.

I gained a fundamental understanding of how powerful geospatial technologies are to support landscape-scale conservation efforts, land management, and wildlife management.
